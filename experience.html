Experience

Jake Belnap

During my internship as a Wet Process Engineering Intern at Texas Instruments, I gained valuable semiconductor manufacturing experience, including running etch rate tests on relevant chemistry and film combinations to track and improve production processes. I developed expertise in Wet & Dry Etch, CMP, CVD, PVD, Photolithography, and Metrology. Additionally, I performed fab-wide material balances, utilizing Excel data analysis and Python coding to provide accounting and corrections of chemical usage.

As a Chemical Engineering Technician at Sustainable Energy Solutions, I assisted engineers in enhancing and monitoring Hydrogen Liquefaction skid machinery to support Cryogenic Carbon Capture. My role involved process flow diagrams, multivariate data analysis, and hands-on equipment design, installation, and maintenance. Furthermore, as a Cleanroom Technician at Brigham Young University, I managed inventory and supported cleanroom operations by troubleshooting and repairing machines, pumps, chillers, and other semiconductor manufacturing tools while maintaining a Class 10 cleanroom environment. Additionally, I served as an Engineering Tutor/Professor Assistant at Maricopa Community Colleges, where I assisted over 900 students in mastering technical engineering concepts and problem-solving skills.
